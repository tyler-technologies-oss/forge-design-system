Punctuation

This guide will help you craft consistent copy specific to the needs of your product teams that fit within the overarching voice and tone of Tyler Tech.

Abbreviations​

Use sparingly and only for broadly familiar abbreviations. They should always be capitalized.

When referencing the name of a Tyler product or service within another product or any client-facing context, use the entire name.

Do

check_circle_outline

"Open Manager Self Service to access information about this employee."

Don't

cancel

"Open MSS to access information about this employee."

Ampersands (&)​

The word “and” should be used first, whenever possible. If the situation does not allow for the entire word, such as in the context of saving space, use an ampersand (&) over the plus sign (+).

Do

check_circle_outline

"Images and attachments"

Don't

cancel

"Images + attachments"

Apostrophes & contractions (')​

Use apostrophes in possessives and contractions.
Use common contractions such as “isn’t or ‘can’t.” Use “could have” instead of “could’ve.”

Do

check_circle_outline

I am not sure if I could have been able to complete that project without the extension.

check_circle_outline

There isn’t a good way to download those files.

Don't

cancel

I could’ve finished that project if I had gotten an extension.

cancel

There is not a good way to download those files.

Capitalization​

Headers and titles​

Use sentence casing for headers and titles (“Headers and titles” instead of “Headers and Titles”). Sentence case improves readability and gives proper nouns their proper emphasis.

For more information comparing sentence and title case, check out this Guide to letter casing.

Do

check_circle_outline

Avoid unnecessary capitalization.

check_circle_outline

Capitalize proper nouns.

check_circle_outline

When multiple names are used together, the common noun associated with them should be lowercase. (York and Cumberland counties)

check_circle_outline

Capitalize product names.

check_circle_outline

Capitalize jurisdictions or entity in the copy when talking about the full official client name, but lowercase when using only “county,” “city,” “town,” etc.

Colons (:)​

Use colons before lists.

Do

check_circle_outline

"The following reports are ready: Monthly Transactions, Quarterly Payroll, and Annual Budget."

Don't

cancel

"Reporting queue finished: needs attention."

Ellipsis (...)​

Use an ellipsis (…) to indicate the deletion of one or more words in condensing quotes, texts, and documents. It may also be used to indicate a process is running.

Do

check_circle_outline

“Welcome. We’re building your experience... You’re all set!”

Don't

cancel

“Would you like to save...?”

First / second person​

Opt for writing in second person (you, your) over first person (we, our) whenever possible to inspire a sense of ownership in our clients.

Do

check_circle_outline

"Use the new feature to..."

Don't

cancel

“Our new feature allows you to...”

Hyphen (-)​

Hyphenate for clarity.

Use lowercase for e-words in the body of a text. Use initial capital letters in headings, titles, and at the beginning of a sentence. Per AP Style, use a hyphen when forming words with self. (Self-contained)

When referencing an actual function, use a hyphen. (self-service site)

Do

check_circle_outline

“She preferred face-to-face interaction with clients, rather than the faceless e-commerce business model.”

check_circle_outline

“E-commerce is a profitable business model.”

check_circle_outline

“How to Succeed in E-Commerce (heading)”

Don't

cancel

“This project requires 20 - 30 hours of work”

Periods (.)​

Do

check_circle_outline

Body text in the UI that is a full sentence.

check_circle_outline

Bullets in a list that are full sentences.

Don't

cancel

Body text that is a fragment

cancel

Titles or headers

cancel

Subtitles or sub headers

cancel

Any text in title case

cancel

Any text styled in all caps

Personal titles​

A persons title should only be capitalized when the title is used directly before the individuals name. Use lowercase when the title is used after their name.

Do

check_circle_outline

Vice President of Sales Tyler Ryan gave a great speech.

check_circle_outline

Tyler Ryan, vice president of sales have a great speech.

Don't

cancel

vice president of sales Tyler Ryan gave a great speech.

cancel

Tyler Ryan, Vice President of sales was unhappy with this quarters projections.

Serial commas (,)​

Use commas to separate words in a list including one before the last word in the list (also known as the Oxford comma). Commas should also be used in numbers greater than three digits.

Do

check_circle_outline

"The following reports are ready: Monthly Transactions, Quarterly Payroll, and Annual Budget."

Don't

cancel

“The following reports are ready: Monthly Transactions, Quarterly Payroll and Annual Budget.”
